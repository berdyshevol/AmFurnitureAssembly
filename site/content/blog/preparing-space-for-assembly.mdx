# 5 Tips for Preparing Your Space Before Furniture Assembly

Whether you are tackling the project yourself or hiring a professional assembler, a little preparation goes a long way. Taking fifteen minutes to set up your space before the first screw gets turned can shave serious time off the job and prevent costly mistakes. Here are five tips we share with every client.

## 1. Clear the Room

This is the single most impactful thing you can do. Furniture assembly requires spreading parts, panels, and hardware across the floor. A cluttered room means constant shuffling, and shuffling means scratched panels and lost screws.

Move small furniture, rugs, and decor out of the area. You do not need to empty the entire room, but aim for at least a 10-by-10-foot clear zone. If the piece is especially large, like a wardrobe or a sectional sofa, you may need even more space. Having room to walk around the item while it is being built makes the process faster and safer.

## 2. Organize All Parts and Hardware

Before assembly begins, open every box and verify the contents against the parts list. Group similar hardware together: bolts in one pile, cam locks in another, dowels in a third. This inventory step catches missing or damaged parts before you are halfway through the build.

We recommend using small bowls, cups, or even a muffin tin to keep hardware sorted. A missing washer discovered at step 47 can turn a two-hour job into a four-hour ordeal if you have to disassemble and reassemble sections.

## 3. Read the Instructions First

It sounds obvious, but most people skip straight to step one without scanning the full manual. Reading through the instructions once gives you a mental roadmap of the build. You will spot steps that require two people, identify which panels look similar but are not interchangeable, and understand the overall sequence.

Pay special attention to any warnings or notes highlighted by the manufacturer. These often flag common mistakes that are difficult or impossible to reverse once the piece is partially assembled.

## 4. Have Basic Tools Ready

Most flat-pack furniture comes with the hex keys and basic hardware you need, but relying solely on those can slow you down. A cordless drill with a Phillips bit will speed up screw-driving dramatically. A rubber mallet helps seat dowels without damaging laminate surfaces.

Here is a short list of tools that make almost any assembly job easier:

- Cordless drill or screwdriver
- Phillips and flat-head bits
- Rubber mallet
- Level (a phone app works in a pinch)
- Tape measure
- Scissors or box cutter for packaging

Having these within arm's reach means you will not lose momentum searching the garage mid-build.

## 5. Protect Your Floors

Assembly involves flipping panels, dragging parts, and occasionally dropping small hardware. Hardwood, tile, and laminate floors are all vulnerable to scratches and dents. Lay down a moving blanket, old bedsheet, or even flattened cardboard boxes from the packaging itself.

This protective layer also gives you a softer surface to kneel on, which your knees will appreciate after an hour of floor-level work. If you are assembling on carpet, the blanket still helps by preventing small screws and dowels from disappearing into the fibers.

## Ready to Go

A prepared space sets the stage for a smooth, stress-free assembly. Whether you handle the build yourself or call in a professional, these five steps ensure the job goes as quickly and cleanly as possible.
